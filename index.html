"Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind"


by Yuval Noah Harari

Publication Date: 2011
Description: A compelling overview of human history, exploring how biology, culture, and technology have shaped our species and societies.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: History, Anthropology
  • Translated into more than 50 languages
  • Combines science, history, and philosophy

"The Alchemist"


by Paulo Coelho
Publication Date: 1988
Description: A philosophical novel about a young shepherd named Santiago who embarks on a journey to discover his personal legend and fulfill his dreams. It’s a timeless tale of adventure, destiny, and self-discovery.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: Fiction, Inspirational
  • Language: Originally written in Portuguese
  • Bestseller: Sold over 65 million copies worldwide

"Atomic Habits"


by James Clear
Publication Date: 2018
Description: A practical guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones through small, incremental changes. It provides actionable strategies rooted in behavioral science.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: Self-help, Productivity
  • Key Feature: Includes frameworks like the Habit Loop and the 1% Rule
  • Highly praised for its clarity and simplicity

"Man’s Search for Meaning"


by Viktor E. Frankl
Publication Date: 1946
Description: Written by a Holocaust survivor and psychiatrist, this book explores the search for purpose and meaning in life, even in the face of unimaginable suffering.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: Memoir, Psychology
  • Language: Translated into over 24 languages
  • Profound insight into the human spirit and resilience

"Meditations"


by Marcus Aurelius
Publication Date: Around 161-180 CE (Modern English editions vary)
Description: A collection of personal writings by the Roman Emperor, offering timeless wisdom on stoicism, self-discipline, and leadership.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: Philosophy, Classics
  • Language: Originally written in Greek
  • A cornerstone of Western philosophical thought

"Thinking, Fast and Slow"


by Daniel Kahneman
Publication Date: 2011
Description: A deep dive into how our minds work, explaining the two systems of thought—fast, intuitive thinking and slow, deliberate reasoning—and their impact on decision-making.
    Additional Details:
  • Genre: Psychology, Behavioral Economics
  • Written by a Nobel laureate in Economic Sciences
  • Provides insights into biases and heuristics
